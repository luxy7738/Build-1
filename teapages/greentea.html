Green Tea

Green tea, celebrated for its delicate flavor and numerous health benefits, is a type of tea that has been enjoyed for centuries. It shares its origins with black tea and oolong tea, all of which come from the Camellia sinensis plant. However, green tea distinguishes itself through its unique processing method, which preserves its natural color and compounds.

The hallmark of green tea is its minimal oxidation. Unlike black tea, green tea leaves undergo a brief withering process, followed by immediate steaming or pan-firing to halt oxidation. This preservation of natural compounds results in green tea's characteristic grassy, vegetal taste and light green liquor.

Beyond its pleasant flavor, green tea is celebrated for its health-promoting properties. It is a rich source of antioxidants, particularly catechins like epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), which have been linked to various potential health benefits. These include aiding metabolism, supporting heart health, and providing antioxidant protection against free radicals.

Green tea comes in various varieties, including sencha, matcha, and jasmine green tea, each offering a unique taste experience. Whether enjoyed as a traditional tea ceremony in Japan or sipped casually worldwide, green tea continues to captivate tea enthusiasts with its refreshing taste and potential health advantages.
