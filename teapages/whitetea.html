White Tea

White tea, celebrated for its delicate flavor and minimal processing, is among the most cherished and gentlest of all tea varieties. It is revered for its exceptional purity and the ethereal experience it offers to tea enthusiasts.

What sets white tea apart is its minimal oxidation. The tea leaves, typically young buds and leaves, are plucked with great care and allowed to wither naturally. Unlike other teas, they undergo minimal processing, which preserves their natural freshness.

The result is a tea that captivates with its subtle, sweet, and slightly floral notes. White tea is often described as having a naturally sweet and mild taste, with delicate hints of apricot, honey, and fresh flowers. Its gentle flavor profile is complemented by a light and almost ethereal aroma.

White tea is not only cherished for its exquisite taste but also for its potential health benefits. It is rich in antioxidants and is believed to support skin health and boost overall well-being. This timeless tea is often associated with relaxation and rejuvenation.

White tea is traditionally enjoyed in a calm and meditative setting, where its subtle nuances can be fully appreciated. Whether you're seeking tranquility or a refined tea experience, white tea invites you to savor the simplicity and elegance of each cup.

Types of White Tea

  • Silver Needle
  • White Peony
  • Shou Mei
  • Gong Mei
  • Darjeeling White
